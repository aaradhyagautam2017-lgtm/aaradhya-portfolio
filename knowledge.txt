— EARLY EXPERIENCE —
In my first year I joined a startup with a couple of seniors, but we realised our
visions didn't match, so I stepped away from the team.

After that, I co-founded a design agency with friends called Designabit. I worked there
as a designer — on strategy, and on onboarding and handling clients. We had three clients:
  1. A gemstone business that creates raw stones from the ashes of loved ones, using
     artificial-diamond technology.
  2. A canned beverage company selling canned water to high-end venues — airports,

  3. An agency that builds websites and software for other businesses.
I eventually left Designabit — as a friend's agency it didn't scale well, and around the
same time my friend and I both got internships. I felt learning how the corporate world
works mattered. As my father says: to start your own company, you first have to learn
how a company functions.

— NOISE: VISUAL DESIGN —
I joined Noise as a Visual Design intern, starting out making watch faces. But I was
tired of doing only visual work — I wanted more, so I asked for it, and I was given
watch UI work.

— NOISE: WATCH UI —
I began by creating screens alongside my seniors, then earned the chance to own the whole
watch UI myself. I spotted a big flaw in the Noise watch ecosystem: the watches came from
different vendors, so there were 8–12 different UIs floating around in the market, which
broke the overall Noise watch experience.

My team and I decided to build a single, unified UI that would work across all the watches.
The challenge was that different watches had different capabilities — TFT vs AMOLED screens,
and varying memory and processors. So I designed a watch UI compatible across the whole
range, in two versions:
  • An animation-dense version for higher-end watches with more computing power.
  • A lighter version for low-end watches — fewer animations, but an almost identical design.
Each of those had a square and a round variant — so four complete watch UI designs in total.

— NOISE: AUDIO APP —
After proving myself, I joined the lead designer on the Noise Audio app. The lead designer
soon left, and I became the sole owner of Noise Audio. My first project was a complete
redesign — the experience was broken and outdated — so we rebuilt all of it: user flows,
journeys, onboarding. Then I noticed major inconsistencies in colours, components, and more,
so I built a complete, Atlassian-level design system for Noise Audio. As AI started taking
off, I wanted to bring AI into our Design Language System (DLS) to deliver front-end faster
and reduce turnaround time and the load on the dev team, so I began integrating it with an agent.

— WHAT I'M LOOKING FOR —
I'm looking for product-focused design work, and I'm especially drawn to mobile apps. I love
owning an app end to end — designing it, handling it, integrating it, and continuously
streamlining its flows. I'm at my best when I take a messy or scattered product and bring
order and structure to it.

— MY STRENGTHS —
Above all, I'm a problem solver. Give me a problem and I'll find the best, most optimal
solution for it. For example, at Noise, to speed up front-end turnaround, I built the entire
design system as a coded file — so with one click you can get any screen ready, and within
about five minutes the full code for a page, screen, or section is done.

— MY VISION —
My bigger goal is to build a streamlined system where designers ship production-ready
front-end code directly, instead of just handing Figma files over to developers. I want to
close the gap between design and development.

— MY DESIGN PHILOSOPHY —
Design, to me, is where creativity meets structure — it lets me be creative while staying
professional. I'm driven by finding problems and engineering the most optimal solution,
bringing order to chaos, and increasingly erasing the line between design and code.
